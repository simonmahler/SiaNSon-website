SIA N'SON MICROFINANCE
About Microfinance

Microfinance programs provide small-scale financial services to people living in poverty, those to whom such services are otherwise unavailable. Small, low interest loans allow the poor to start or expand businesses and achieve goals that would otherwise not be feasible. Because many borrowers cannot provide the lending institution with monetary or physical collateral, microfinance institutions typically rely on group solidarity to enforce loan reimbursement. The group as a whole is held responsible for every member's monthly payments. This mechanism helps to ensure a low rate of defaulted loans.

Microfinance programs are generally extremely efficient and to a large extent economically self-supportive. Their impact on the economy and ability to function without the financial support of a third party has made them a popular method for fighting world poverty. They are not without problems however. The transition from opening a roadside stand to operating a small or medium-sized enterprise, for example, is often difficult to make. Sia N'Son Microfinance, in particular, is working to find ways to help clients overcome these obstacles. Through its provision of a host of services, each catering to the individual needs of borrowers, Sia N'Son is truly working for a better tomorrow.


Our History and Mission

Founded in 1995, Sia N'Son is a registered Non-Governmental Organization (ONG) located in Parakou, Benin. Sia N'Son works alongside partners PNUD, Planete Finance, Catholic Relief Services, and Banque Regionale de Solidarite to pursue its mission of promoting economic development in underprivileged communities. Microfinance is only one part of a larger organism that facilitates adult literacy education and sets the stage for new economic activity. With nearly 44% of its population under the age of 15 and approximately a third of its citizens below the poverty line, Sia N'Son is playing an important role in Benin’s socioeconomic growth.
